# Retrospective Facilitation Playbook

A guide to facilitating effective sprint retrospectives that drive continuous improvement and team growth.

## Purpose

The retrospective is the team's dedicated time for reflection and improvement. A well-facilitated retro:
- Creates psychological safety for honest discussion
- Identifies what's working and what isn't
- Generates actionable improvement items
- Strengthens team bonds and communication
- Builds a culture of continuous learning

## Retrospective Principles

### The Prime Directive

Before every retro, read the Prime Directive:

> "Regardless of what we discover, we understand and truly believe that everyone did the best job they could, given what they knew at the time, their skills and abilities, the resources available, and the situation at hand."
> — Norman Kerth

### Core Principles

1. **Blameless**: Focus on systems, not individuals
2. **Constructive**: Aim for improvement, not venting
3. **Inclusive**: Every voice matters
4. **Action-oriented**: End with concrete commitments
5. **Safe**: What's said in retro stays in retro

## Retrospective Structure

### Standard Flow (60-90 minutes)

| Phase | Duration | Purpose |
|-------|----------|---------|
| Opening | 5-10 min | Set the stage, review actions |
| Gathering Data | 15-20 min | Collect observations and feelings |
| Generating Insights | 20-25 min | Identify patterns and root causes |
| Deciding Actions | 15-20 min | Commit to specific improvements |
| Closing | 5-10 min | Appreciate and wrap up |

### Phase 1: Opening (5-10 minutes)

**Set the Stage**

1. Welcome everyone, state the purpose
2. Remind of safety agreements (Prime Directive)
3. Review action items from last retro
4. Set expectations for this session

**Icebreaker Options**:
- One word to describe the sprint
- Rate the sprint 1-10 (anonymous)
- Weather report: What's your current weather?
- Emoji check-in

### Phase 2: Gathering Data (15-20 minutes)

**Collect individual perspectives before group discussion**

Popular formats:
- Start/Stop/Continue
- What Went Well / What Didn't / Ideas
- Mad/Sad/Glad
- Liked/Learned/Lacked/Longed For (4Ls)
- Sailboat (Wind, Anchor, Rocks, Island)

**Tips**:
- Use silent writing first (3-5 minutes)
- One topic per sticky note
- Everyone participates
- No judgments during collection

### Phase 3: Generating Insights (20-25 minutes)

**Move from observations to understanding**

1. **Cluster similar items**: Group related topics
2. **Dot voting**: Prioritize what to discuss
3. **Discuss top items**: Use "5 Whys" for root cause
4. **Identify patterns**: What's recurring?

**Facilitation Questions**:
- "What's really behind this?"
- "When did this start happening?"
- "What would have prevented this?"
- "Is this a symptom of something bigger?"

### Phase 4: Deciding Actions (15-20 minutes)

**Convert insights into commitments**

**Good Action Items**:
- Specific and measurable
- Have a clear owner
- Can be done next sprint
- Address root causes

**Examples**:

| Insight | Poor Action | Good Action |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| "Too many meetings" | "Have fewer meetings" | "Cancel Wednesday standup, try async update this sprint" |
| "Unclear requirements" | "Get better requirements" | "Add 15-min PO walkthrough at refinement start" |
| "Technical debt slowing us" | "Fix tech debt" | "Reserve 10% capacity for debt this sprint" |

**SMART Action Template**:
```
Action: [What specifically]
Owner: [Who]
Due: [When]
Success: [How we'll know it worked]
```

### Phase 5: Closing (5-10 minutes)

**End on a positive, forward-looking note**

Options:
- Appreciation circle: Thank a team member
- One-word checkout: How are you leaving?
- Return on Time Invested (ROTI): Was this valuable?
- Plus/Delta: Quick feedback on the retro itself

## Retrospective Formats

### 1. Start/Stop/Continue

Classic format for any team:
- **Start**: What should we begin doing?
- **Stop**: What should we stop doing?
- **Continue**: What's working that we should keep?

### 2. Sailboat

Visual metaphor for team journey:
- **Island** (goal): Where are we headed?
- **Wind** (helps): What's pushing us forward?
- **Anchor** (holds back): What's slowing us down?
- **Rocks** (risks): What hazards are ahead?

### 3. 4Ls

Comprehensive reflection:
- **Liked**: What did you enjoy?
- **Learned**: What did you discover?
- **Lacked**: What was missing?
- **Longed For**: What do you wish you had?

### 4. Mad/Sad/Glad

Emotion-based (good for stuck teams):
- **Mad**: What frustrated you?
- **Sad**: What disappointed you?
- **Glad**: What made you happy?

### 5. Timeline

Sprint chronology (good for complex sprints):
1. Draw timeline of the sprint
2. Mark significant events (good and bad)
3. Add emotions at each point
4. Discuss patterns and pivots

### 6. Lean Coffee

Participant-driven agenda:
1. Everyone writes topics (2 min)
2. Vote on what to discuss
3. Discuss top topic (5 min)
4. Thumbs vote to continue or move on
5. Repeat until time's up

### 7. Team Radar

Skills/practices assessment:
- Draw radar chart with 5-8 dimensions
- Team rates each dimension 1-5
- Discuss biggest gaps
- Plan improvements

Dimensions example:
- Communication
- Technical Practices
- Collaboration
- Velocity
- Quality
- Morale

## Remote Retrospective Tips

### Tools

- **Miro/Mural**: Virtual whiteboard with templates
- **Retrium/EasyRetro**: Purpose-built retro tools
- **FunRetro**: Simple and free
- **Jamboard**: Google's whiteboard

### Best Practices

1. **Camera on**: Encourage video for engagement
2. **Smaller groups**: Use breakout rooms for large teams
3. **More time**: Allow extra time for remote logistics
4. **Silent writing**: Use first to prevent dominant voices
5. **Clear turns**: Use explicit facilitation for speaking order
6. **Check connectivity**: Have backup plan for tech issues

## Facilitator Tips

### Before the Retro

- [ ] Book room/video call with buffer time
- [ ] Prepare materials (boards, stickies, markers)
- [ ] Review previous retro actions
- [ ] Choose appropriate format
- [ ] Plan icebreaker

### During the Retro

- Protect the quiet voices
- Keep discussions on track
- Watch the clock
- Stay neutral (don't dominate)
- Take notes or assign a scribe
- Ensure psychological safety

### After the Retro

- [ ] Document action items in team backlog
- [ ] Share summary (if appropriate)
- [ ] Follow up on actions during sprint
- [ ] Prepare for next retro

## Handling Difficult Situations

### Silent Team

**Signs**: No one speaks, minimal participation
**Causes**: Fear, fatigue, or trust issues

**Solutions**:
- Use anonymous input collection
- Start with small group discussions
- Use more structured activities
- Check in privately with individuals
- Address potential safety issues

### Dominant Voices

**Signs**: Same people always speak, others withdraw
**Causes**: Personality differences, hierarchy

**Solutions**:
- Use round-robin for speaking
- Silent writing before discussion
- Breakout groups
- Direct facilitation: "Let's hear from someone who hasn't spoken"

### Blame Game

**Signs**: Finger-pointing, defensiveness
**Causes**: Fear, lack of safety

**Solutions**:
- Re-read Prime Directive
- Redirect to systems, not people
- "What process could have prevented this?"
- Private conversation with blamers

### No Actions

**Signs**: Good discussion but no commitments
**Causes**: Overwhelm, lack of ownership

**Solutions**:
- Limit to 1-3 actions maximum
- Assign specific owners
- Choose small, achievable items
- Add actions to sprint backlog immediately

### Repeated Issues

**Signs**: Same problems every retro
**Causes**: Root cause not addressed, lack of follow-through

**Solutions**:
- Use "5 Whys" to go deeper
- Review why past actions didn't work
- Escalate systemic issues
- Involve management if needed

## Measuring Retro Effectiveness

### Track Action Completion

- How many actions were completed?
- Did actions solve the intended problems?
- Are issues actually improving?

### Team Health Metrics

- Team morale over time
- Velocity trends
- Quality metrics
- Team satisfaction surveys

### ROTI (Return on Time Invested)

At end of retro, ask team:
```
How valuable was this retro? (1-5)
1 = Waste of time
2 = Somewhat useful
3 = Average
4 = Valuable
5 = Extremely valuable
```

Target: Average 4+

## Sample Retro Agendas

### Quick Retro (30 min)

```
1. Check-in (3 min)
2. What Went Well / What Didn't (10 min silent + 5 min share)
3. Vote and discuss top item (10 min)
4. One action item (2 min)
```

### Standard Retro (60 min)

```
1. Opening + review last actions (10 min)
2. Gather data: Start/Stop/Continue (15 min)
3. Cluster and vote (5 min)
4. Discuss top 3 items (20 min)
5. Define 2-3 actions (8 min)
6. Closing appreciation (2 min)
```

### Deep Dive Retro (90 min)

```
1. Check-in + safety check (10 min)
2. Timeline of sprint (20 min)
3. Pattern identification (15 min)
4. Root cause analysis (20 min)
5. Action planning (20 min)
6. Closing + ROTI (5 min)
```
